Preface

Self-concept is the sum of the ways in which we describe ourselves, while identities are the individual components of our self-concept, often shaped by and related to the groups to which we belong.

I find it difficult to articulate the very concept of self-concept in just a few sentences. Therefore, I have decided to forgo the conventional introduction detailing my past and present affiliations, commitments, and achievements on the front page (those interested may refer to the Curriculum Vitae). Instead, I am going to list some of my failed or perhaps still ongoing attempts to understand myself and the world here.

  • I often find it difficult to figure out what I am doing at the present moment, and refuse to think about what I am going to become in the future, partly out of subjective panic and partly because of the objective complexity of the question.
  • I find the notion of perfection somewhat deceptive and, in this respect, feel an affinity towards mysticism.
  • I was once a patriot, then politically apathetic, and now actively ponders the idea of good governance.
  • I assimilate into the groups with which I identify rather quickly, but frequently worry about the loss of identities previously established.
  • I believe humans possess a natural inclination to acquire information in the most efficient way possible, and I attribute my myopia, internet addiction, and cynicism to this tendency.
  • I am curious about the biological basis of various mental conditions and personality disorders, and believe in biomedical approaches towards their remediation.
  • I hope to make a tangible difference to the places in which I live, rather than pursuing wealth, prestige, or recognition for their own sake.
  • I value concrete thinking and genuine innovation over gilding work already established by others or harvesting the low-hanging fruits of thought, though reality has repeatedly suggested that the latter may well be my forte.
  • I am an ambitious leader who often feels that the crowd has failed my expectations, only to find myself in constant doubt over my own capacity afterwards.
  • I enjoy solitude most of the time, yet on Saturday nights I do long for companionship and conversations that stretch late into the night.

Finally, cheers to anyone who has taken the time to read these rather naïve and fragmented thoughts. I hope you enjoy navigating this site.

To the freedom of thinking, expression, and being.
